# Code of Conduct

`thia.lol` should be a safer, weirder, kinder social space. That starts with the project itself.

## Expected behavior

Contributors, testers, and maintainers are expected to:

- be respectful and constructive
- assume good faith where reasonable
- give feedback about work, not personal worth
- respect boundaries, names, pronouns, and identities
- avoid sharing private or sensitive information
- report bugs without shaming the person who made them
- help beginners without acting like knowledge came bundled with the human body at birth

## Unacceptable behavior

The following is not welcome:

- harassment, threats, stalking, or intimidation
- slurs, hate speech, or dehumanizing language
- racism, sexism, homophobia, transphobia, ableism, or other targeted bigotry
- sexual comments or advances in project spaces
- outing, doxxing, or sharing private information
- posting private messages, credentials, tokens, cookies, or sensitive user data
- intentionally derailing issues or pull requests
- repeated hostile, mocking, or bad-faith behavior

## Project spaces

This applies in:

- GitHub issues and pull requests
- project discussions
- linked community spaces
- bug reports
- maintainer/contributor conversations about the project

## Reporting

If something happens, contact the maintainer privately when possible. If you do not have a private contact path, open a minimal public issue asking for one without including sensitive details.

Reports will be handled with as much care and privacy as possible. Depending on the situation, maintainers may remove comments, close issues, block users, or restrict participation.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take action when behavior harms contributors, testers, users, or the project. Consequences may include a warning, comment removal, issue locking, temporary restriction, or permanent ban from project spaces.

The goal is not performative politeness. The goal is making it possible for people to contribute without needing emotional armor and a legal department.
